## Mental Models as a Compressible Knowledge Format

### Summary
Mental models — cross-domain abstractions that explain how things work — are the most efficient format for storing and retrieving knowledge. A single mental model (e.g., compounding, inversion, margin of safety) can apply across investing, career, health, relationships, and learning. The key insight: identify the abstract pattern, strip the domain-specific details, and store the pattern generically. Domain-specific applications become instances of the pattern, not separate knowledge entries.

### Evidence Chain
- Source 1: Charlie Munger's "latticework of mental models" approach — confidence: 0.85
- Source 2: Observed pattern: investment analysis frameworks (DCF, margin of safety) map to non-finance domains (career decisions, project estimation) — confidence: 0.80

### Key Mental Models (Domain-Agnostic)
1. **Compounding**: Small consistent gains over time → exponential results (applies to: investing, learning, fitness, relationships)
2. **Inversion**: Instead of "how to succeed," ask "how to fail, then avoid that" (applies to: risk management, project planning, personal goals)
3. **Margin of Safety**: Leave room for error beyond what you think is necessary (applies to: time estimates, financial decisions, commitments)
4. **Circle of Competence**: Know what you don't know; stay within the boundary (applies to: career, analysis, giving advice)
5. **Feedback Loops**: Output → input → adjusted output; systems either self-correct or spiral (applies to: habits, teams, markets, learning)
