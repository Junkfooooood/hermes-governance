## Morning Routine Experiment — Silence vs. Input

### Hypothesis
Starting the day with 30 minutes of silence (no podcasts, no news, no phone) improves focus and decision quality in the first 2 hours of work, compared to starting with information consumption.

### What I'm Testing
- **Condition A**: Morning commute + first 30 min in silence → measure focus quality at 9:00-11:00
- **Condition B**: Morning commute + first 30 min with podcasts/news → same measurement
- **Duration**: 7 days alternating (A/B/A/B/A/B/A)
- **Metric**: Subjective focus score (1-10) at 11:00 + number of "deep work" sessions before noon

### Observations So Far
- Day 1 (A - silence): Focus 7/10, 1 deep work session. Felt less rushed, more intentional about what to work on first.
